# Taxes for Craft Businesses

## Tax Obligations Overview

| Tax Type | What It Is |
|----------|------------|
| **Federal income tax** | Tax on profit (Schedule C if sole prop/LLC) |
| **State income tax** | Varies by state |
| **Self-employment tax** | 15.3% on net profit (Social Security + Medicare) |
| **Sales tax** | Collected from customers, remitted to state |
| **Quarterly estimated taxes** | Required if you'll owe $1000+ annually |

## Sales Tax

### Who Collects

- **Physical presence states**: Collect in your home state
- **Economic nexus states**: Collect where you exceed thresholds (~$100K sales or 200 transactions)
- **Etsy collects for most states**: Marketplace facilitator laws mean Etsy handles collection/remittance

### If Selling Outside Etsy

- Own website, craft fairs, direct sales
- You may need to register in states where you have nexus
- Oregon has no state sales tax

### Sales Tax Checklist

1. Register for seller's permit in home state (if applicable)
2. Know whether Etsy collects for you (they do in most states)
3. Keep records of where sales go
4. File returns even in months with no sales (if registered)

## Deductible Business Expenses

### Direct Costs (COGS)
- Blank garments
- Dyes, chemicals, soda ash
- Packaging materials
- Shipping supplies

### Operating Expenses
- Etsy fees, PayPal fees
- Website hosting
- Software subscriptions
- Office supplies
- Professional development (courses, books)

### Home Office (if dedicated space)
- Percentage of rent/mortgage interest
- Percentage of utilities
- Percentage of internet

### Equipment
- Tables, racks, buckets
- Camera for product photos
- Computer (business use percentage)

### Marketing
- Advertising costs
- Business cards
- Craft fair booth fees
- Samples

### Vehicle (if applicable)
- Mileage to post office, supply runs, craft fairs
- Standard mileage rate: ~$0.67/mile (check current IRS rate)

## Record-Keeping Requirements

**What to track**:
- All income (Etsy deposits, direct sales, wholesale)
- All expenses with receipts
- Mileage log (date, destination, miles, purpose)
- Inventory (beginning/ending)

**Tools**:
- Wave Accounting (free, good for beginners)
- QuickBooks Self-Employed (~$15/month)
- Simple spreadsheet works if disciplined
- Separate business bank account is essential

**How long to keep records**: 7 years minimum

## Quarterly Estimated Taxes

**Who pays**: If you expect to owe $1000+ in taxes for the year

**Due dates**:
| Quarter | Due Date |
|---------|----------|
| Q1 | April 15 |
| Q2 | June 15 |
| Q3 | September 15 |
| Q4 | January 15 |

**How much**: Safe harbor = 100% of last year's tax liability (110% if AGI > $150K)

**Form**: IRS Form 1040-ES (federal) + state equivalent

## Self-Employment Tax

15.3% of net profit:
- 12.4% Social Security (on first ~$160K)
- 2.9% Medicare (no cap)

**Note**: You can deduct half of SE tax on your 1040.

## Tax Calculation Example

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Gross revenue | $30,000 |
| COGS | -$8,000 |
| Operating expenses | -$5,000 |
| **Net profit** | **$17,000** |
| Self-employment tax (15.3%) | $2,601 |
| Federal income tax (~12%) | $2,040 |
| **Total tax liability** | **~$4,641** |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not separating business/personal**: Makes audit risk higher, deductions questionable
2. **Missing deductions**: Track everything, especially small purchases
3. **Forgetting quarterly payments**: Underpayment penalties add up
4. **Not tracking inventory**: Required for COGS calculation
5. **Ignoring hobby loss rules**: If no profit in 3 of 5 years, IRS may classify as hobby (lose deductions)

## When to Get Professional Help

**Consider a CPA or tax pro when**:
- Revenue exceeds $50K
- Considering S-corp election
- First year of business (setup right)
- You're getting audited
- Multi-state sales tax complexity

**Cost**: $200-500 for basic Schedule C return

- [ ] Compile 1099s received

- [ ] Tally mileage log
- [ ] Review for missed deductions
